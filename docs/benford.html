🔍What is Benford’s Law?

Although the mathematical property was first noted by Simon Newcomb, today’s widely known Benford’s Law is a curious statistical principle used in anomaly detection. It reveals that in many real-world datasets, small leading digits (i.e. 1) are counterintuitively more likely to occur than large digits. This patterns seems to be present in a wide range of cases, ranging from:

  • 🗳️ election results,

  • 🏡 street addresses,

  • 📊 financial data,

  • ⚰️ mortality stats,

  • 🧪 to molecular weights.

## 📐 Generalised Benford’s Law: The Formula Behind the Pattern
The probability that a number begins with digit d (from 1 through 9) is:
\[ P(d) = \log_{10} \left(1 + \frac{1}{d} \right) \]
Where:

  • \(P(d)\) is the probability that the first digit is \(d\)

  • The logarithm is base-10

  • This formula shows why 1 appears about 30.1% of the time, while 9 only shows up around 4.6%
